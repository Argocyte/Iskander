# Iskander: Lunarpunk Cooperative Infrastructure

**A White Paper on People, Place, and Planet**

*April 2026*

---

## Abstract

Cooperatives are the democratic alternative to extractive capitalism. They are governed by members, share surplus equitably, and prioritise community over profit. Yet the digital tools cooperatives rely on -- Google Workspace, Slack, Zoom -- are built for hierarchies, not democracies. They concentrate data in corporate hands, undermine member privacy, and extract value from the communities they claim to serve.

Web3 promised decentralisation but delivered speculation and plutocracy. Regenerative Finance (ReFi) claims to heal the planet but academic evaluation finds 45% of projects are conventional sustainability repackaged with tokens (Frontiers in Blockchain, 2025). The cooperative movement deserves better.

Iskander is lunarpunk cooperative infrastructure. It is a federated platform that puts web3 in the hands of people who otherwise wouldn't know how to touch it, in a decision-making and project-planning environment that just works. It uses cryptography not for speculation but for five specific cooperative problems that traditional technology cannot solve: tamper-evident decisions, anti-coercion voting, Sybil-resistant identity, intermediary-free inter-cooperative trust, and anti-extractive finance.

The thesis is simple: generous tit-for-tat is the optimal game theory strategy in iterated games with noise. A federated cooperative economy built on this principle will develop emergent behaviours that outcompete extractive capitalism over time. Iskander is the infrastructure that makes this possible.

## 1. The Problem: Coordination Failure

### 1.1 Cooperatives Without Cooperative Tools

The International Co-operative Alliance (ICA) represents over 3 million cooperatives worldwide, employing more than 280 million people -- 10% of the global workforce. These organisations are founded on seven principles: voluntary membership, democratic member control, economic participation, autonomy, education, cooperation among cooperatives, and concern for community.

Yet when a housing cooperative needs to decide whether to accept a new member, they use Google Forms. When a worker cooperative needs to vote on pay distribution, they use email threads. When cooperatives want to trade with each other, they rely on the same banks and payment networks that extract 2-3% from every transaction.

The digital infrastructure cooperatives use actively undermines the principles they stand for:

- **Google Workspace** centralises all cooperative data on corporate servers, violating Principle 4 (Autonomy and Independence)
- **Slack** charges per user, creating economic barriers to participation, violating Principle 1 (Voluntary and Open Membership)
- **Zoom** records meetings on corporate infrastructure, with no member control over data retention
- **PayPal/Stripe** extract transaction fees that could be surplus returned to members, violating Principle 3 (Member Economic Participation)

### 1.2 Web3's Broken Promise

The blockchain movement promised to decentralise power. Instead, it created new forms of extraction:

- **Token-weighted governance** gives more votes to those with more capital -- the antithesis of one-member-one-vote
- **Speculative tokenomics** attract investors seeking returns, not members seeking cooperation
- **"Decentralised" platforms** concentrate power in core development teams and whale token holders
- **Gas fees** create participation barriers that exclude lower-income community members

A 2025 Frontiers in Blockchain study evaluated 40 ReFi projects from a database of 564. Results: 50% genuinely regenerative, 45% "Sustainable DeFi" (conventional sustainability repackaged with tokens), 5% structurally misaligned. The core warning: "without grounding in systems theory and ecological economics, ReFi replicates extractive logics under innovation branding."

A companion paper warns that "blockchain enables unprecedented commodification, potentially making the Global Commons the next, and final, commodity frontier through over-securitisation and collateralisation of natural assets."

### 1.3 The Missing Layer

What cooperatives need is not "blockchain" or "web3" as currently marketed. They need infrastructure that:

- Is **owned and controlled** by the cooperative, not a vendor
- Enforces **one-member-one-vote** at the protocol level
- Makes **decisions transparent** to members but **private from the world**
- Enables **inter-cooperative trade** without banks, courts, or platform intermediaries
- Works for people who have **never heard of Ethereum** and never will

## 2. The Thesis: Emergent Cooperative Advantage

### 2.1 Game Theory of Cooperation

In 1984, Robert Axelrod's iterated prisoner's dilemma tournaments demonstrated that cooperative strategies dominate in repeated interactions. The winning strategy -- generous tit-for-tat (GTFT) -- follows three rules:

1. **Cooperate first** (optimistic initial stance)
2. **Mirror** the other party's last move (reciprocity)
3. **Forgive defection 5-33% of the time** (prevent death spirals from noise)

The "generous" element is critical. In real-world interactions, "noise" -- miscommunication, late payments, resource shortfalls, honest mistakes -- is constant. Pure tit-for-tat punishes every defection, leading to retaliatory spirals. GTFT's probabilistic forgiveness absorbs noise while still deterring exploitation.

For inter-cooperative economics, this translates directly: a cooperative that fails to deliver once should not be permanently blacklisted. But a cooperative that repeatedly defects should face declining trust and escalating consequences.

### 2.2 From Individual to Emergent

Research consistently finds that federated cooperative networks develop emergent properties that individual cooperatives lack:

- **Collective intelligence**: Diverse nodes sense market and regulatory shifts faster than any single cooperative. A housing cooperative in Manchester detects a policy change affecting worker cooperatives in Leeds.
- **Resource resilience**: Each local node can access the broader network's expertise, capital, and surplus capacity. A small cooperative weathering a crisis draws on the federation's collective reserves.
- **Trust as a system property**: Trust emerges from norms of reciprocity and repeated interaction across the network, not from individual bilateral relationships. It becomes a commons maintained by all.
- **Anti-fragility**: Unlike centralised organisations that have single points of failure, federated networks strengthen under stress as failing nodes are supported and their functions distributed.

### 2.3 Historical Proof

This is not theoretical. The Mondragon Corporation in the Basque Country is a federation of over 80 cooperatives with 65,000+ worker-owners. It has operated for 70 years, surviving multiple economic crises, because the federation provides mutual support: cooperatives in difficulty receive capital and members from cooperatives in surplus. The Emilia-Romagna region of Italy has the highest density of cooperatives in Europe and consistently ranks among Italy's wealthiest regions.

What these federations lack is digital infrastructure designed for their operating model. They coordinate through phone calls, spreadsheets, and legacy enterprise software built for hierarchical firms. Iskander provides the missing layer.

## 3. The Vision: Lunarpunk Cooperative Infrastructure

### 3.1 Why Lunarpunk, Not Solarpunk

Solarpunk envisions a sustainable civilisation in harmony with nature, powered by renewable technology and cooperative governance. Adam Flynn argues that "there's an oppositional quality to solarpunk, but it's an opposition that begins with infrastructure as a form of resistance."

Iskander embraces this vision. But we build from the lunarpunk critique.

Rachel-Rose O'Leary's lunarpunk thesis warns that solarpunk's transparent systems build their own prison. Surveillance capitalism will co-opt any system that makes individual behaviour visible. The deeper critique is that building identity-based systems is "intrinsically statist because it follows the Western rationalist logic of Gestell -- slowly turning people into documented stock, controllable with bureaucracy."

For cooperatives, this matters concretely: in a worker cooperative, your colleagues are also your employers. Open ballot voting creates social pressure -- members may vote to please a dominant personality rather than express genuine preference. Transparent contribution tracking can become a surveillance tool rather than a coordination mechanism.

**Iskander resolves the solarpunk/lunarpunk tension through selective disclosure:**

| Layer | Visibility | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|-----------|
| Aggregate outcomes | Transparent to members | "Decision passed 7-2" in Glass Box |
| Individual votes | Always secret | MACI zero-knowledge proofs |
| Treasury flows | Transparent to members | On-chain records via Clerk |
| External visibility | Opaque by default | ZK proofs verify without revealing |
| AI actions | Transparent to members | Glass Box logs every action |

The cooperative's collective integrity is verifiable without exposing individual members to surveillance. This is the DarkFi AnonDAO principle applied to cooperative governance.

### 3.2 Infrastructure as Resistance

Iskander is not "blockchain for cooperatives." It is **cooperative infrastructure that happens to use cryptography where it uniquely solves problems.** The distinction matters.

A cooperative installs Iskander and gets: decision-making (Loomio), real-time chat (Mattermost), an AI assistant (the Clerk), shared files and calendar (Nextcloud), a password vault (Vaultwarden), automatic backups (Backrest), system monitoring (Beszel), and single sign-on (Authentik). All self-hosted, all behind one login. No blockchain knowledge required.

The web3 layer operates underneath, invisible to most members: decisions are automatically anchored to an immutable ledger; sensitive votes use zero-knowledge cryptography; inter-cooperative trade settles without payment network fees; reputation scores decay exponentially, forcing continuous engagement.

Members interact with the Clerk: "What proposals are pending?" "Help me draft a motion for the AGM." "How much is in our treasury?" The Clerk translates between human intent and cooperative infrastructure. It never votes, never advocates, never decides. It helps members make their decisions.

## 4. Five Cryptographic Solutions

Each web3 component in Iskander exists because it solves a specific cooperative problem that traditional technology cannot. No component exists for speculative, branding, or "innovation theatre" purposes.

### 4.1 Tamper-Evident Decision Records

**Problem**: Cooperative decisions stored in a database can be silently altered by any administrator. For an organisation governed by democratic mandate, this is a structural vulnerability.

**Solution**: When a Loomio poll closes, the decision payload is pinned to IPFS (a content-addressed storage network where the address is derived from the content itself -- any change produces a different address). The IPFS hash is then recorded on an append-only blockchain ledger. No party, including the cooperative's own system administrators, can retroactively alter the record without detection.

**ICA Alignment**: Principle 2 (Democratic Member Control) requires that decisions be accountable. Tamper-evident records make accountability verifiable, not just promised.

### 4.2 Anti-Coercion Voting

**Problem**: Open ballot voting in workplace cooperatives creates social pressure. Members may vote to please a dominant personality rather than express genuine preference. This is a known failure mode in cooperative governance.

**Solution**: MACI (Minimum Anti-Collusion Infrastructure) provides encrypted voting with zero-knowledge tallying. It is the only known system that simultaneously achieves:
- Proof that only eligible members voted
- Mathematical proof that the tally is correct
- Impossibility of linking any individual to their vote
- Resistance to vote-buying (because the buyer cannot verify the seller's vote, via a "last message wins" mechanism)

**ICA Alignment**: Principle 2 requires "equal voting rights (one member, one vote)." MACI ensures that equality is substantive, not merely formal -- every vote carries equal weight because no vote can be externally verified or coerced.

### 4.3 Sybil-Resistant Identity

**Problem**: One-member-one-vote requires proving each member is a unique human. Email verification is trivially attackable (anyone can create multiple addresses). Traditional KYC requires government-issued identification, which is extractive, invasive, and excludes undocumented people -- violating Principle 1 (Voluntary and Open Membership "without gender, social, racial, political or religious discrimination").

**Solution**: Soulbound Tokens (SBTs) are non-transferable, non-purchasable on-chain records of membership. Combined with BrightID's peer-to-peer social graph verification (where real humans vouch for each other without government ID or wealth signals), this creates Sybil resistance without KYC.

**ICA Alignment**: Principle 1 requires membership "open to all persons able to use their services and willing to accept the responsibilities of membership." BrightID + SBTs enforce this without discriminatory barriers.

### 4.4 Inter-Cooperative Trust Without Intermediaries

**Problem**: When two cooperatives trade, who holds escrow? Who arbitrates disputes? In the traditional economy, banks extract fees, courts are slow and expensive, and platform intermediaries accumulate power. ICA Principle 6 demands cooperatives work together, but working together requires trust infrastructure.

**Solution**: Three interlocking contracts create intermediary-free inter-cooperative commerce:
- **IskanderEscrow**: Funds are locked by smart contract code (not a bank) until both parties confirm delivery
- **ArbitrationRegistry**: Disputes are resolved by federated human juries drawn from uninvolved cooperatives (not a court)
- **ForeignReputation**: Trust scores decay exponentially with a 30-day half-life, creating GTFT dynamics. Trust must be continuously earned through interaction. No cooperative can rest on historical reputation alone.

**ICA Alignment**: Principle 6 (Cooperation among Cooperatives) and Principle 4 (Autonomy). Cooperatives trade on their own terms without intermediary dependency.

### 4.5 Anti-Extractive Finance

**Problem**: Payment card networks (Visa, Mastercard) extract 2-3% from every transaction. For worker cooperatives operating on thin margins, this is extractive rent. Additionally, cooperatives need mechanisms to enforce equitable pay ratios and protect indivisible reserves.

**Solution**: CoopFiatToken (cFIAT) is a 1:1 fiat-backed token enabling inter-cooperative settlement without payment fees. InternalPayroll enforces Mondragon-style pay ratio caps at the contract level -- the ratio cannot be violated even by a compromised administrator. The StewardshipLedger's circuit breaker prevents financial exposure from exceeding reserves.

**ICA Alignment**: Principle 3 (Member Economic Participation) states that "capital is the servant, not the master." cFIAT tokens are non-speculative (1:1 fiat-backed, non-tradeable on exchanges), indivisible reserves are protected by contract, and surplus distribution requires democratic approval via Loomio.

## 5. Values Alignment

### 5.1 ICA Cooperative Principles as Design Constraints

Every technical decision in Iskander is constrained by the seven ICA Cooperative Principles. These are not marketing -- they are hard requirements.

| Principle | Constraint | Implementation |
|-----------|-----------|----------------|
| P1: Voluntary & Open Membership | No discriminatory barriers | BrightID (no KYC), accessible UX, AI Clerk as equaliser |
| P2: Democratic Member Control | One-member-one-vote, accountable representatives | MACI ZK voting, Loomio governance, tamper-evident records |
| P3: Economic Participation | Capital as servant; indivisible reserves; limited returns | Non-speculative SBTs, contract-enforced reserves, cFIAT |
| P4: Autonomy & Independence | No external control or dependency | Self-hosted K3s, Headscale mesh, sovereignty-first design |
| P5: Education & Training | Members can contribute effectively | AI Clerk as personal tutor, proactive governance training |
| P6: Cooperation among Cooperatives | Work together through structures | GTFT federation, escrow, reputation, Headscale mesh |
| P7: Concern for Community | Sustainable development | Energy-aware scheduling, SDG tracking, environmental accountability |

### 5.2 Learning from DisCO

The Distributed Cooperative Organization (DisCO) framework, developed by Guerrilla Translation, expands cooperative principles with feminist economics. DisCOs track three value streams:

- **Livelihood work**: Paid productive output
- **Love work**: Care, emotional labour, community maintenance -- "often invisibilised and gendered"
- **Pro-bono commons work**: Contributions to shared resources

Iskander's Clerk agent is designed to help cooperatives track and value all three streams, not just "productive" output. When a member spends time onboarding a new member, mentoring a colleague, or mediating a conflict, this is valuable cooperative work that should be visible and appreciated.

### 5.3 Learning from Hypha

Hypha DAO's evolution from DAO 1.0 (code-centric) through DAO 2.0 (protocol-optimised) to DAO 3.0 (human-centred) contains a critical lesson: "governing the machine" fails. Governance is "an emergent property, one that must dynamically evolve as context shifts."

Iskander takes this lesson seriously. The blockchain records and enforces. Humans deliberate and decide. Loomio is where the human conversation happens. Smart contracts are the notary, not the judge.

### 5.4 Learning from ICN

The InterCooperative Network project identifies that "identity, rules, decisions, obligations, execution, proof -- should be one coherent substrate rather than seven tools held together by staff discipline." ICN is "trust-native, not trustless" -- trust operates as an auditable system property rather than something designed away.

Iskander shares this philosophy. Trust between cooperatives is not eliminated by cryptography -- it is made verifiable and accountable through it.

## 6. For People, Place, and Planet

### 6.1 People

**Every member gets an AI clerk.** This is the most radical equality feature in Iskander. In traditional cooperatives, members with more education, confidence, or free time dominate governance. The Clerk equalises: it explains proposals in plain language, helps draft motions, summarises long discussions, reminds members of pending votes, and guides new members through governance training.

The Clerk embodies ICA Principle 5 (Education, Training, and Information). It never votes, never advocates, never decides. It helps every member participate fully in the democratic life of their cooperative.

**Privacy protects the vulnerable.** Zero-knowledge voting exists because in a workplace cooperative, voting against a popular manager's proposal should not carry professional consequences. In a housing cooperative, voting against a neighbour's request should not create social friction. Lunarpunk privacy is not anti-transparency -- it is pro-safety.

### 6.2 Place

**Self-hosted means community-controlled.** Every Iskander instance runs on hardware the cooperative controls -- a VPS they rent, a server in their office, a Raspberry Pi cluster in a community centre. No corporation can unilaterally change terms of service, raise prices, or discontinue the platform.

**Community-scale, not global-scale.** Solarpunk's vision of "little nests of self-sustaining revolution" (Adam Flynn) is architecturally embodied in Iskander's design. Each cooperative's K3s cluster is a sovereign unit. Federation through Headscale mesh creates inter-cooperative connectivity while preserving local autonomy.

**Local economics first.** The escrow and reputation systems are designed for cooperatives trading with their neighbours -- the worker cooperative that supplies the housing cooperative that houses the consumer cooperative's members. Global platforms extract local value upward; Iskander keeps value circulating locally.

### 6.3 Planet

**Environmental accountability is structural.** Iskander uses proof-of-stake (Anvil/EVM), not proof-of-work. The energy-aware scheduler (from the archived Iskander Hearth specification) enables cooperatives running on renewable energy to schedule computationally intensive tasks -- AI inference, blockchain operations -- during periods of high renewable generation.

**SDG tracking is built in.** The community impact module maps cooperative activities to the 17 UN Sustainable Development Goals using the ICA's "Co-ops for 2030" framework. Cooperatives can demonstrate their community contribution with data, not just aspiration.

**Commons, not commodities.** The ReFi critique warns against "making the Global Commons the next commodity frontier." Iskander's web3 layer does not tokenise natural assets, create carbon markets, or financialise ecosystem services. It provides cooperative infrastructure. The distinction is fundamental.

## 7. Technical Architecture

### 7.1 The Stack

Iskander runs 15 services on a single server (16GB RAM recommended) orchestrated by K3s:

| Layer | Services |
|-------|----------|
| **Orchestration** | K3s (lightweight Kubernetes) |
| **Identity** | Authentik (SSO/OIDC) |
| **Governance** | Loomio (proposals, votes, decisions) |
| **Communication** | Mattermost (real-time chat) |
| **AI** | OpenClaw + Ollama (Clerk and Steward agents) |
| **Files** | Nextcloud (documents, calendar, contacts) |
| **Data** | PostgreSQL + Redis + IPFS Kubo |
| **Operations** | Vaultwarden, Backrest, Beszel |
| **Network** | Cloudflared + Headscale |

Phase B adds: Anvil (EVM), Chain Bridge, Stalwart (email), Caddy (website), Dendrite (Matrix).

### 7.2 Installation

```bash
curl -sfL https://get.iskander.coop/install | sh -s -- \
  --coop-name "Sunrise Workers" \
  --admin-email "founder@sunrise.coop"
```

One command. Ansible provisions K3s, deploys Helm charts, generates secrets, creates accounts, and opens the browser. Your cooperative is ready.

### 7.3 Federation

Each cooperative's K3s cluster is a sovereign node. Headscale creates an encrypted WireGuard mesh between cooperatives. The ForeignReputation contract tracks inter-cooperative trust with exponential decay (30-day half-life), creating generous tit-for-tat dynamics across the federation.

No central authority. No platform intermediary. No extraction.

## 8. Call to Action

### For Cooperatives

Iskander is free and open source software (AGPL-3.0). If your organisation is governed by the principle of one-member-one-vote, it is designed for you:

- Worker cooperatives
- Housing cooperatives
- Consumer cooperatives
- Platform cooperatives
- Community land trusts
- Credit unions
- Producer cooperatives

### For Developers

Iskander is built on mature FOSS tools (Loomio, Mattermost, Nextcloud, K3s) with custom integration. Contributions are welcome. The codebase is structured for clarity: each service in its own directory, each agent skill in its own SKILL.md, each smart contract independently deployable.

### For the Movement

The cooperative movement has demonstrated for 180 years that democratic enterprise works. What it has not yet built is digital infrastructure designed for democratic enterprise. Iskander is that infrastructure -- lunarpunk, federated, and built for people, place, and planet.
